Atari Go

Scala

Atari Go is a simplified version of the traditional board game Go. This board game is played by one player using black stones and the opponent using white stones. The first player to capture a predefined number of the opponent's stones wins the game.
Players take turns placing one stone on the board, and passing a turn is not allowed. Stones can only be placed on the intersections of the board lines that are not already occupied. Once placed, a stone cannot be moved.
A capture occurs when a player's stones completely surround (not considering diagonals) one or more of the opponent's stones, meaning that the captured stone(s) have no remaining liberties. It is invalid to play on intersections that have no liberties. A stone cannot be placed in such a way that the group it belongs to is left with no liberties, unless this move results in the capture of one or more of the opponent's stones.

Fruit Catcher Game

Python

This project aims to explore the application of artificial neural networks in controlling complex systems by evolving a feed-forward neural network to play the Fruit Catcher game.
The goal of the game is to catch the good fruits while avoiding the bombs (which are disguised as fruits). The objective is to optimize the performance of the neural network using a genetic algorithm. Additionally, you should train a decision tree classifier to identify which fruits are safe to catch.
